Ashia Ackov Contemplates Love and Legacy in Jazz Ballad "In Time"

The following feature is now included in our online magazine which is also available in print.

Issue #3

Online Magazine | Print Magazine

For more details contact us at: volechomag@gmail.com

Ashia Ackov's latest single, "In Time," is an introspection on memory, love, and the subdued burden of sacrifice. Going back to the legacy of Jazz Noire, the song finds inspiration in Ackov's grandparents and the tale of the 92nd Infantry soldiers who abandoned their brides when they shipped out for war. That tug-of-war between faith and doubt—"Do I ever get to see you again? Is it worth it?"—is the emotional center of this song, making it rich with depth that resonates long after the last note has dissolved. The music from the first chord is imbued with introspective warmth. Piano melodies roll along in subdued determination, conjuring waves, and muted brass provides illuminating wisps of soft, wistful light onto the story. An insistent rhythm propels the song along like a pulse, anchoring the listener in the force of chronology in the tale. Ackov's voice remains at the forefront, unbroken and slow, each line charged with history, desire, and a quiet hope that bridged past to present.

Listening to "In Time" is like reading a movie, the feeling of being ushered into one of the classic black-and-white films where a bride gestures farewell while a soldier slumps out of the window of an open train and remains suspended in tender promise. It's what makes this song so beautiful: its narrative. Ackov's music was always textured, but in this piece, the storytelling is simple and suggestive. The song doesn't just tell a phenomenon—s/he invites the audience to live it.

For music lovers who like music that fuses history and emotion, "In Time" will touch a chord, being a class of modern jazz ballads paying tribute to individual and shared memory, like Norah Jones' reflective songs or Gregory Porter's storytelling tradition. On Spotify, YouTube, Apple Music, and Amazon, "In Time" is a listen and commentary on measuring love and legacy. For emotionally charged, movie-inspired jazz fans, the song is one to listen to, giving way to a moment of pause for stories that are both personal, historical, and universal.
