A Moment of Reverence: Eddy Mann’s “Oh Come” Brings Fresh Warmth to a Timeless Carol

January 17, 2026 by Volecho.com

Follow Our Playlist For More Music!

The following feature is now included in our online magazine which is also available in print.

Issue #10

Online Magazine | Print Magazine

"OH COME" slotted right in with the rest of Mann’s oeuvre, which extends over two decades and two dozen albums. Mann’s reputation for fusing various cultures and spiritual traditions into music that inspires without having to resort to showmanship in the process translates into a track like "OH COME," which presents a moment for introspection that is both contemporary and eternal.



For those who enjoy Contemporary Christian music, worship, and singer-songwriter performances of holiday standards, "Oh Come" is a natural fit for holiday playlists and worship services. The strength of the song is in its subtlety: it is a song that invites the listener to pause, listen, and respond to the invitation to honor the season.

The gentle voice and production of Mann make the song as fitting for personal listening as it is for corporate worship, distilling the heart of the Christmas story without pretense. It is an important reminder that sometimes the most powerful moments of music and faith come through the power of spontaneity and simplicity.

What Eddy Mann has created with "Oh Come" is something far greater than a Christmas song; it is a musical moment which captures the quiet joys and reflections at the heart of the season. This is music which is certain to live on year after year as both celebration and meditation.
